About me

Denish Azamuke aims for leadership in financial inclusion using Artificial Intelligence. He is currenly focusing on a practical approach for automated fraud detection in mobile money transactions from Sub-Saharan Africa.

Due to privacy concerns, the law and the high sensitivity of fiancial transactions, the research aims to first harness synthetic mobile money transactions datasets before the actual fraud modeling and detection can take place.

Prior to Synthetic financial data generation, he worked on a financial modeling project that forecasted the volatility of forex market returns in Rwanda forex market so as to inform investors about which currencies they can invest in.

This research focused on the dominant currencies in the market that influence the development of the country (Rwanda) and they include; the Great British Pound, US Dollar, Euro, Ethiopian Birr, Kenyan Shillings, and Tanzanian Shillings.

During his undergraduate studies at Makerere University, he developed API intensive mobile application that runs on android platform to enable motor cyclists (Boda-boda riders) and motorists to access fuel on credit from nearby petrol stations. Payments in the application were handled using mobile money technology.

This solution came towards addressing the problem of motorists not having cash to purchase fuel in order to start their journeys or they simply run out of fuel while on trips.

Projects and Conferences

Synthetic Mobile Money Transaction Datasets

This projects aims to generate synthetic mobile money transaction datasets for Machine Learning (ML) Reasearch. The idea is to harness synthetic mobile money transaction datasets that are as close as possible to original mobile money transaction data. With the synthetic datasets, research teams can continue with AI research in the domain of financial fraud detection without any data privacy concerns.

Once the synthetic datasets have been evaluated to have thesame analytical qualities as the real mobile money datasets, then automated fraud detection is the following step that will eventually solve the problem of fraud in financial systems in Sub-Saharan African and hence promoting Financial Inclusion in the region.

Value-at-Risk Forecast for Forex Market Returns in Rwanda

This project was about the idea in finance that investors in forex markets are generally interested in knowing for certainty the amount of their wealth that will not be lost over a certain period. This implies that the knowledge of the Value-at-Risk (VaR) is paramount to investors in order to commit themselves to invest in forex markets. In Rwanda forex market, investing in hard dominant currencies is attractive for large investments.

To guarantee investors in this line about the increase of their wealth invested, there was need to forecast the VaR measure for each currency return based on the Pick-Over-Threshold (POT) approach and the Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity (GARCH) method to provide the most efficient method that could be used by any investor in the market to determine the market risk.
